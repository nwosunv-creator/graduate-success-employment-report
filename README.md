This report examines graduate success and employment trends to assess how effectively graduates transition into the workforce after completing their studies. It analyzes employment rates, sectors of employment, and time to secure jobs, highlighting key patterns and changes over time

This report examines graduate success and employment trends to assess how effectively graduates transition into the workforce after completing their studies. It analyzes employment rates, sectors of employment, and time to secure jobs, highlighting key patterns and changes over time. The findings provide valuable insights for institutions, policymakers, and graduates to better align education outcomes with labor market demands

Methodology

This analysis was done in microsoft excel where Raw data from graduate surveys and institutional records were imported into Excel, where data cleaning was performed to remove duplicates, correct errors, and standardize entries. Pivot tables and charts were used to summarize employment status, industry distribution, and time to employment. Key metrics were calculated using formulas and percentage analysis to identify trends and support data-driven conclusions

post analysis observation

The law profession records a starting salary of $46093.75 with an employability score of 22, concentrated within the GPA ranges of 2.8-3.0 and 3.0-3.2, male graduates reporting higher career satisfaction, and a qualification outcome of $51181, indicating moderate employability supported by certifications but limited by lower GPA distribution.

The Business field of study records an average starting salary of 81,486.11, which places it in the mid-tier salary benchmark when compared to other fields such as Computer Science and Engineering. Employability in this discipline stands at 184, showing a relatively strong demand for business graduates. Additionally, career satisfaction is highlighted under the male gender category, with an average career value of 134,000, indicating stable long-term career progression and professional contentment among male graduates in this field

post analysis recommendations

Strengthen experiential learning through internships, case competitions, and industry mentorships to sustain employability growth.

Promote inclusive satisfaction strategies, ensuring female graduates also experience high levels of career satisfaction by addressing workplace flexibility, leadership opportunities, and equity

Expand capacity in Computer Science programs to meet growing industry demand without compromising quality, given the strong employability scores.

Encourage interdisciplinary learning, integrating computer science with business, healthcare, and engineering to create multi-skilled graduates who can command even higher career opportunities

Leverage technology integration by embedding digital health, AI diagnostics, and health informatics into medical curricula to prepare graduates for evolving career opportunities.

Expand clinical training capacity through partnerships with hospitals and teaching institutions to ensure employability growth matches the rising demand

Encourage postgraduate and specialized certifications (e.g., critical care, oncology, informatics) to improve qualification outcomes and competitiveness.

Strengthen leadership and policy engagement opportunities for female nurses to enhance career satisfaction and visibility in decision-making roles
